Triceratops

This is a collection of facts that I found interesting about this funky-looking dinosaur. While I was listening to the Terrible Lizards Podcast by Dr David Hone and Iszi Lawrence I found myself taking notes and then I wanted to explore this topic futher.


Notes

  • End of Creteceous (last mio. years of dinosaurs)
  • North America
  • Sympatric with T-Rex (= same time, same place)
  • Biggest of the Certosians (horned dinosaurs)
  • Most specimens are way bigger than what we see in museums: larger than rhinos (around 2m sholider height)
  • Solid bones: very heavy
  • Back legs longer than front legs, probably because ancestors were bipedal (happend often)
  • Ancestors had spiky cheeks and frill
  • Bipedal animals have their center of mass above the hips: balance point
  • Quadrupeds have centre of mass in front of their hind legs
  • Pachyrhinosaurus: big, lumpy horn
  • Triceratop's frill is solid bone, different from the other ceratopsians
  • Ceratopsians may have had filament, one specimen definitely had it
  • Triceratops could have had filament along its back, from large pointy scales, known from a specimen with skin in Kanada
  • Triceratops was fully herbivorous, large beak
  • Efficient plant-digester because of its size
  • Beaks have evolved from very different animals; beak = bony part in front of the face, usually made up of keratin
  • Triceratops' teeth have two roots (v-shape), unusual and unique, not found in any other reptile
  • A single two-rooted tooth has been found in Bulgaria, otherwise rarely any specimens in Europe: why so rare?
  • Ceratosians not found in Europe, but in America and Asia, even though the distance would be easy to travel back then
  • Horns used for self defence, but not exclusively
  • One specimen of Triceratops found where one brow-horn has T-Rex tooth marks on it
  • Carnivores hunt young ones; safer and easier since if wounded likely to die of starvation and therefore avoided fights
  • Triceratops horns for fighting with each other (see: antilope, deer, etc)
  • Study of where Triceratops would hit and lock into each other: skeletons (of both genders) are injured mostly in those areas, likely because of territory control
  • Some ceratopsians lived in groups (floods show mass drownings), they get semi-regularly trapped in groups
  • No groups of Triceratops though, unless young (probably solitary when older)
  • If horns were critical for self-defence, babys would grow them very quickly becuase they're more often predated, but this is not the case
  • Shield mostly hollow in most ceratopsians, and thus likely not actually armor. It also covers the wrong bit of body and even that not very well against predators
  • Hind legs most vulnerable, especially when running away
  • Torosaurus: largest terrestrial head ever found, discussion if it's an evolution of Triceratops (like grasshopper - locust), but this is not generally agreed on
  • Torosaurus and Triceratops have not been found together or in the same vicinity
  • The shield of Triceratops gets thinner in the middle, it could theoretically be possible that they could open up completely when much older
