Introduction

My name is Fanny Schreck and I am currently pursuing a bachelor's degree in environmental engineering at the Vienna University of Technology and my master's degree in history at the University of Vienna. Since this is only one of my interests, I am currently also dabbling in a few other areas, such as computer science (web design, typesetting systems and data compression) as well as research on teaching and learning practice.
I also enjoy furthering my knowledge through conversation and discussion, one of my reasons for launching a debate club in my school and becoming its president. I have found that regularly reading challenging texts and writing papers on topics of interest to me is an involving way to dive deeper into them and consolidate my understanding. I don't think there is a lot to be gained by dwelling only on theory if you never put it into practice. I probably owe my personal approach to learning to my weakness for books - even today you hardly see me without one and I still enjoy devouring one in a single afternoon from time to time...

Writing

During my history bachelor's degree I have had the opportunity to create many works concerning a multitude of different topics, themes and historical periods. Some of these were written out of my interest to gain a deeper understanding of a particular topic or theory, while others are attempts at working with new methods.

Speaking & Roles

Having completed the International Baccalaureate (IB) with exceptionally high points, I was invited to local IB schools and preparatory tutoring institutions to give talks about my personal experience and share study-tips. I have also been invited to help prepare and give my own lessons as an auxiliary teacher in one of these IB schools as a result.

Tutoring

Giving talks in various IB-institutions enabled me to connect to many teachers and students. If needed, I would then give tutoring lessons in subjects that I graduated in. After being exposed to many different students, I could experiment first-hand with teaching strategies and forms of explanation. This prompted to do more research into those aspects of education science that I thought to be valuable for teaching strategies to understand texts and bigger conceptual ideas.
