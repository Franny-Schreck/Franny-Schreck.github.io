Work Experience

Since 2017, I've regularly done holiday internships - from which I have learned valuable lessons about working as part of teams of different sizes, backgrounds and motivations. Alongside working on my bachelor's degree in history I also took some jobs on a part-time basis, including an auxiliary teaching position at an IB school and customer service. For further details, read on!

Background

My educational journey started in a Gymnasium (grammar school) in the Austrian countryside, which I attended until I finished the Unterstufe (graduate level). After spending a gap year in a 'Waldorf' school in England, I switched to an International Baccalaureate school in Vienna. I am very glad I made this decision because it allowed me to experience an approach to the subjects taught and relationships with the teachers that I had not come across thus far.
Following a series of summer jobs in administrative office environments and seasonal waiting in restaurants, I was able to pursue something that I felt to be more impactful: helping to teach students and encouraging them to fulfill their potential as an auxiliary teacher.
I am looking forward to working in academic research positions in the course of my master's degree in history as well as any that will emerge from my current pursuit of Environmental Engineering!

Bachelor in Environmental Engineering

I've always been interested in the complex interactions of environmental science and engineering, so that I had already enrolled in a few courses about this during my history bachelor. I am very excited to be studying this!

Customer Service at Wien Ticket

Here, I mostly interacted directly with custormers through one of the many Wien Ticket booths, although I also helped out in office positions, answering more complex queries via e-mail and working with the accounting team.

Bachelor in History

I got my degree in 2023, with a grade point average of 1.28 and thus passed with distinction. The pursuit of this degree taught me many valuable lessons, including the importance of asking interesting questions in a way that tentative answers can be gained through thorough research, balanced argumentation, and good presentation.

Auxiliary teaching role in German and history lessons in the International Highschool Herzogberg

I was very fortunate to be given the opportunity to work with the amazing teaching staff and even help with planning lessons for the upper level classes to which I could contribute my perspective as a recent student.

Talk at Study Academy Vienna

Having completed the International Baccalaureate (IB) with exceptionally high points, I was invited to give a talk on learning strategies and an explanation of the various different courseworks required at the Study Academy Vienna during one of their IB Revision Courses.

International Baccalaureate at Campus Wien West

I was very fortunate to study in this bilingual school in Vienna, where I graduated with an International Baccalaureate and 44 out of 45 points, resulting in a grade point average of 1.0 when converted to the Austrian grading system.

Holiday internship at the IB&C Ges.m.b.H.

Here my main job was of an administrative nature, organising files on a computer and sorting paperwork. This enabled me to further my Microsoft Excel and Word skills, and made me grateful for the ECDL-licence I obtained while in school.

Internship (Talente Praktikum) at the Core Facility Cell Imaging and Ultrastructure Research (CIUS) Institute of the University of Vienna

During this internship by the Austrian Research Promotion Agency FFG I was able to gain practical experiences in scientific research by working alongside professionals as well as developing and experimenting with topics of my own interests. The result of my experiments was a detailed lab report on the effect of metal toxicity on different kinds of mosses.

Holiday internship in the restaurant 'Honobono'

This was my first 'real' job, and the fast-paced environment was certainly something to get used to. But through keeping up with the customers' wishes and demands as well as helping out in the kitchen after my shift I gained experiences that are still valuable to me today.
