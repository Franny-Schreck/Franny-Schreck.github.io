Background

My educational journey started in the Gymnasium (grammar school) in the countryside, where I would go until I finished the Unterstufe (graduate level). But after spending a gap year in England, I switched to an International Baccalaureate school in Vienna. I am very glad I made this decision because it allowed me to experience a different approach to the subjects taught, which focussed on interdisciplinarity and independent thought as well as a collegial form of relationship with the teachers that encouraged asking questions - even outside the curriculum - something I had not come across thus far.
Following a series of summer-jobs in administrative office environments and seasonal waiting in restaurants, I was able to pursue something that I felt to actually be impactful: helping to teach students and encouraging them to fulfill their potential as an auxiliary teacher.
